I am pursuing a PhD in Computer Science at the University of British Columbia, supervised by Frank Wood. My research areas are probabilistic programming, machine learning, and their application in real-world problems. I am specifically interested in Bayesian inference and (conditional) generative modeling using deep learning and how these two frameworks may be combined by leveraging probabilistic programming.

In 2018, I graduated with an MSc. in Mathematical Modelling and Computation focusing on machine learning and signal processing at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU). Here my research focused on feature extraction in conjunction with Bayesian non-parametrics. Supervised by Morten Mørup, I wrote my master thesis on non-negative matrix factorization and the Indian Buffet Process. In 2016 I received my BSc. in Earth and Space Physics and Engineering at DTU, where i researched acoustic levitation of particles, supervised by Henrik Bruus.
