I am pursuing a PhD in Computer Science at the University of British Columbia. My research areas are probabilistic programming and machine learning, and supervised by Frank Wood I focus my research towards real-world applications. I am specifically interested in Bayesian inference and (conditional) generative modeling and how these two frameworks may be combined by leveraging probabilistic programming.

In 2018 i graduated with an MSc. in Mathematical Modelling and Computation focusing on machine learning and signal processing at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU), where I did research on feature extraction and Bayesian non-parametrics, which was supervised by Morten Mørup. In 2016 I received my BSc. in Earth and Space Physics and Engineering at DTU, where i researched acoustofluidics, specifically acoustic levitation of particles, which was supervised by Henrik Bruus.
